DO WE NEED A NEW HOME?

THE QUESTION

3 kids down to none living at home. What are we going to do with all this extra space? Maybe it's time to start looking for some place else. What about North Carolina? That seems nice...

WHERE, WHEN, WHAT?

THE DECISION

The family has decided on a new family house. After much deliberation over where the house should be, when they will be moving, what type of house they want, and many more important questions, They decide on an Appalachian styled home in the North Carolina town of Asheville. "It's perfect" they exclaim.

IS THIS REALLY HAPPENING?

THE MOVE

Well, it's really happening! The moving truck is packed and the family makes off toward their new city, state, and way of life. They're sad watching their previous home of 25+ years fade in the mirror, but they are excited for change.

THIS IS IT!

THE REVEAL

The family has finally made it to their new house and it is just how they imagined (and hoped) it would be. All those sleepless nights and days of planning ruminating and mulling over the smallest details -- what color shutters they wanted was a huge discussion -- has been worth it. A new chapter has started while another has closed. Almost simultaneously, they say, "We're ...

THE HOUSE IS NOW HOME

HOME

HOME!". The house is now a home, their home. It feels different and new at first. Things like forgetting where the light switches are located and what items go where are common at first, but it doesn't last. Soon, the farmiliar home cadence is restored and things are almost as they always were. Even the family pets get used to the new space and it's not long before they find their favorite sleeping spots again. The journey brings up an interesting thought. Is where you live or where you are?
